GST Registration & Compliance

📝 Overview (What is it?)

Goods and Services Tax (GST) is India's unified, single indirect tax structure for businesses. Obtaining a GSTIN (GST Identification Number) is a crucial requirement that legally permits you to buy, sell, and provide services across the country.

The Power of Professional Credibility: Having a valid GST number instantly upgrades your business profile. Large corporate firms, multi-national vendors, and established suppliers strictly deal only with GST-registered businesses. Without it, you cannot pitch for big corporate B2B contracts.

Claim Back Your Taxes (Input Tax Credit): This is the biggest financial benefit of GST. When you buy raw materials, office equipment, laptops, or professional services for your business, you pay GST on them. If you are registered, you can claim that tax money back (Input Tax Credit) to offset the taxes you owe on your sales, saving your business massive amounts of cash.

E-Commerce Mandatory Entry: If you plan to sell any products online via marketplaces like Amazon, Flipkart, Myntra, or your own Shopify website, getting a GST registration is a mandatory requirement from day one.

👥 Eligibility (Who needs it?)

You must register for GST if your business falls into any of these categories:

  • Service Providers: If your total annual business turnover/revenue crosses ₹20 Lakhs (₹10 Lakhs for Special Category/Hill states).
  • Goods Sellers/Traders: If your total annual sales turnover crosses ₹40 Lakhs (₹20 Lakhs for Special Category states).
  • Interstate Businesses: If you sell goods to a customer located in a different state from your home state, you need it regardless of turnover (even if your annual sales are ₹0).
  • Online E-commerce Marketplaces: Anyone selling products through an online platform must register from day one.
