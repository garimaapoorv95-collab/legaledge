Trademark Registration

📝 Overview (What is it?)

Your brand name, logo, or tagline is the unique identity of your business. A Trademark (TM) is a powerful legal asset that gives you exclusive, permanent ownership over your brand identity. It prevents competitors, copycats, or any third party from using a similar name or logo to steal your customers.

The Power of the ™ and ® Symbols: The moment we file your application, you can legally start using the ™ symbol next to your brand. Once the government fully registers it, you earn the right to use the elite ® (Registered) symbol.

Asset Creation: A registered trademark is a piece of intellectual property. It can be sold, franchised, licensed out for royalties, or used to build massive brand valuation over time.

👥 Eligibility (Who can apply?)

  • Any individual person, freelancer, or solo creator.
  • Any registered business structure (Sole Proprietorship, Partnership Firm, LLP, OPC, or Private Limited Company).
  • Note: Startups and MSMEs holding an active Udyam Certificate get an amazing 50% discount on government filing fees.

📁 Documents Required (What paperwork is needed?)

  • Logo / Brand Name Copy: A clear digital image of the logo or wordmark you want to protect.
  • ID & Address Proof: PAN Card and [Aadhaar Redacted] of the applicant (or corporate authorization if applying under a company name).
  • User Affidavit: If you have been using the brand name for months or years before applying, we need a simple signed proof showing the date you first started using it.
  • MSME / Udyam Certificate: (Optional, but highly recommended to claim the 50% government fee discount).

⚡ The Process (How does it work?)

  1. Step 1: Public Trademark Search: Before filing, we perform a deep, comprehensive search through the official government intellectual property database to ensure your chosen name doesn't clash with existing registered brands.
  2. Step 2: Selecting Trademark Classes: We analyze your business model to classify your products or services into the correct categories out of the 45 global Trademark Classes.
  3. Step 3: Application Filing (Form TM-A): We draft and submit the formal application online to the Trademark Registry. You can instantly start using the ™ symbol on your website and products.
  4. Step 4: Examination & Publication: Government examiners review the file. Once satisfied, they publish your mark in the official Trademark Journal for 4 months to ensure no one objects to it.
  5. Step 5: Registration Certificate: If no valid objections arise, the government issues your permanent digital Trademark Registration Certificate.

Service Details: Patent Filing

📝 Overview (What is it?)

A Patent is an exclusive, sovereign legal right granted by the government for a brand-new invention. It gives you absolute protection over your unique technology, mechanical invention, software algorithm, or chemical formula, making it illegal for anyone else to manufacture, import, or sell your invention without your written permission.

Market Monopoly: A patent grants you a strict 20-year monopoly over your invention. This means you can entirely block competitors from copying your hard work or license your technology to multi-national corporations for massive royalty streams.

👥 Eligibility (Who can apply?)

An invention must fulfill three strict legal conditions to qualify for a patent:

  • Novelty: The invention must be entirely new and never published anywhere globally before your filing date.
  • Inventive Step (Non-Obviousness): It cannot be a basic modification. It must feature a technical advancement that is not obvious to a person skilled in that exact field.
  • Industrial Applicability: The invention must be capable of being physically manufactured or used practically in an industry.

📁 Documents Required (What paperwork is needed?)

  • Invention Disclosure Document (IDD): A detailed write-up explaining what your invention does and the specific problem it solves.
  • Technical Drawings/Flowcharts: Visual blueprints, mechanical designs, or operational flowcharts showing how the invention works.
  • Applicant Details: PAN Card, [Aadhaar Redacted], or corporate incorporation data of the inventor/company.
  • Provisional Specification Draft: (If your invention is still in the developmental or testing stage).

⚡ The Process (How does it work?)

  1. Step 1: Patentability Search: We conduct an exhaustive global search across international patent databases to make sure no similar invention has been registered anywhere in the world.
  2. Step 2: Provisional vs. Complete Specification: If your invention is still a work-in-progress, we file a Provisional Patent to secure your global priority date. Once finalized, we file the Complete Specification detailing every technical claim.
  3. Step 3: Patent Drafting & Filing: Our specialized patent attorneys draft the legal patent application, framing your "Claims" carefully so competitors cannot find loopholes. We submit the application to the Indian Patent Office.
  4. Step 4: Publication & Examination: The patent application is officially published by the government. We then file a formal Request for Examination (RFE) to prompt patent officers to review the technicality.
  5. Step 5: Grant of Patent: After clearing any First Examination Reports (FER) and addressing clarifications, the patent is officially granted for 20 years.
